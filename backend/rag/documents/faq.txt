Retail Sales Industry FAQ

Q1. What is retail sales?
Retail sales refer to the sale of goods and services directly to customers for personal use.

Q2. What is a retail sales forecasting system?
A retail sales forecasting system predicts future sales using historical transaction data, trends, and machine learning models.

Q3. Why is sales forecasting important in retail?
Sales forecasting helps businesses manage inventory, reduce losses, optimize staffing, and improve decision-making.

Q4. What factors affect retail sales?
Retail sales are affected by holidays, weather, promotions, customer demand, pricing, seasonality, and economic conditions.

Q5. What is inventory management?
Inventory management tracks stock levels, product movement, and warehouse availability to avoid overstocking or shortages.

Q6. What is SKU in retail?
SKU stands for Stock Keeping Unit. It is a unique identifier assigned to each product.

Q7. What is customer segmentation?
Customer segmentation divides customers into groups based on purchasing behavior, demographics, or preferences.

Q8. What is Average Order Value (AOV)?
Average Order Value is the average amount spent by customers per transaction.

Q9. What is demand forecasting?
Demand forecasting predicts future customer demand for products.

Q10. What is shrinkage in retail?
Shrinkage refers to inventory loss caused by theft, damage, or administrative errors.

Q11. What are the common payment methods in retail?
Common payment methods include cash, credit cards, debit cards, UPI, wallets, and net banking.

Q12. What is dynamic pricing?
Dynamic pricing automatically changes product prices based on demand, competition, and market conditions.

Q13. What is point of sale (POS)?
POS is the system used to process customer purchases and manage transactions.

Q14. What is a retail recommendation system?
A recommendation system suggests products to customers based on purchase history and preferences.

Q15. What is a loyalty program?
A loyalty program rewards repeat customers with discounts, points, or exclusive benefits.

Q16. What is stock replenishment?
Stock replenishment is the process of refilling inventory when stock levels become low.

Q17. What is seasonal demand?
Seasonal demand refers to changes in product demand during specific times of the year.

Q18. What is retail analytics?
Retail analytics uses data analysis to improve sales, operations, and customer experience.

Q19. What is sales trend analysis?
Sales trend analysis identifies patterns and changes in sales performance over time.

Q20. What is customer retention?
Customer retention measures a company’s ability to keep existing customers over time.

Q21. What is cross-selling?
Cross-selling encourages customers to buy related or complementary products.

Q22. What is upselling?
Upselling encourages customers to purchase higher-value products or upgrades.

Q23. What is supply chain management?
Supply chain management handles product sourcing, manufacturing, transportation, and delivery.

Q24. What is basket analysis?
Basket analysis identifies products frequently purchased together.

Q25. What is an out-of-stock situation?
An out-of-stock situation occurs when a product is unavailable for customers.

Q26. What is markdown pricing?
Markdown pricing reduces product prices to clear inventory or increase sales.

Q27. What is return on investment (ROI)?
ROI measures profitability compared to investment costs.

Q28. What is customer lifetime value (CLV)?
CLV estimates the total revenue generated from a customer during their relationship with a business.

Q29. What is omnichannel retail?
Omnichannel retail integrates online and offline shopping experiences for customers.

Q30. What is AI in retail sales?
AI in retail sales helps with forecasting, customer support, recommendation systems, fraud detection, and inventory optimization.
